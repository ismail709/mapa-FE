MAPA offers several training courses for pilots.

PPL Training

Training's goal

The goal of this training is to bring student pilots to the level of skill and experience allowing them to obtain the private pilot’s license.

Conditions of the admission

In order to be admitted to private pilot training, the candidate must meet the following conditions: Be 17 years old on the day of issue of the license. Hold a class 2 medical certificate.

Privileges

This private pilot license allows its holder to exercise, without remuneration, the functions of co-pilot or captain on any aircraft used for non-revenue flights.

CPL Training

Training's goal

The goal of this practical training is to train pilots with the theoretical prerequisites for obtaining the professional pilot license in visual flight conditions (VFR). This training lasts an average of six months.

Conditions of the admission

The conditions for admission to this CPL training are: Be 18 years of age on the day the CPL license is issued. Hold a secondary education baccalaureate in a scientific or technical series or an equivalent recognized document Hold a class 1 medical certificate. Hold a private pilot’s license with a valid rating. Hold a theoretical ATPL or theoretical CPL certificate or be undergoing theoretical ATPL training within MAPA.

Privileges

This commercial pilot license allows its holder: to exercise without remuneration, the functions of co-pilot or captain on any aircraft used for non-revenue flights. to act as pilot-in-command of any aircraft performing a flight other than a commercial transport flight; to perform the duties of pilot-in-command, in commercial air transport, of any airplane whose minimum certified flight crew is one pilot fulfill the duties of co-pilot, in commercial air transport, on board airplanes where the presence of a co-pilot is required.

IR Training

Training's goal

The objective of this practical training is to train pilots who have the theoretical prerequisites for obtaining the IR qualification (instrument flights). This training lasts on average three months.

Conditions of the admission

The conditions for admission to IR training are: Hold a class 1 medical certificate. Hold an aircraft pilot’s license issued by the DAC. Hold a theoretical ATPL or theoretical IR certificate or be in the process of theoretical ATPL training within MAPA.

Privileges

The instrument flight qualification allows its holder to perform the functions of co-pilot or captain on aircraft flying day and night under IFR (Instrument Flight Rules) and IMC (Instrument Meteorological Conditions), subject to that all conditions relating to the license and type rating held by the interested party are also met.

SEP Training

Training's goal

This training aims to enable candidates to acquire the skills necessary to carry out flights on SEP class aircraft and thus obtain this class qualification.

Conditions of the admission

The conditions for admission to this training are: Hold an airplane pilot’s license. Have a total of 70 hours of flight time as captain.

Privileges

The SEP (Single Engine Piston) class qualification allows you to exercise the privileges of your license on a single engine piston aircraft.

MEP Training

Training's goal

The main purpose of this class qualification lies in the management of asymmetrical flight in the event of engine failure.

Conditions of the admission

The conditions for admission to this training are: Hold an airplane pilot’s license. Have a total of 70 hours of flight time as captain.

Privileges

The MEP (Multi Engine Piston) class qualification allows you to exercise the privileges of your license on a multi-engine piston aircraft.

FI Training

Training's goal

The objective of this course is to enable pilot candidates to obtain the flight instructor qualification for the PPL phase. This qualification allows them to instruct pilot trainees in the practical PPL phase. The FI course (PPL) allows the candidate to acquire flight and ground training techniques, in accordance with established teaching methods.

Conditions of the admission

The conditions for admission to the FI-PPL internship required by MAPA comply with those set by national regulations, in particular decree 3163 and its technical regulations annexed to it. These conditions are: Hold at least a PPL(A) Private Airplane Pilot license. Have completely and satisfactorily completed an appropriate general education course approved by the civil aeronautics department. Total at least 200 hours of flight time, including at least 150 hours as pilot-in-command. Pass the oral interview and the in-flight or simulator assessment.

Privileges

Provide flight and ground instruction with a view to issuing a PPL private pilot license.

IRI Training

Training's goal

An IRI instructor qualification allows you to acquire the instrument flight instruction qualification.

Conditions of the admission

In accordance with the technical regulations relating to the theoretical and practical training of flight crew members, before starting an instrument flight instructor (IRI) qualification course, the pilot must: Hold the FI/CPL instructor qualification. Have completed at least 300 hours as pilot-in-command in instrument flight.

Privileges

The IRI qualification allows you to carry out flight instruction for private pilot licenses (PPL) or commercial pilot licenses (CPL) on IR qualifications.

License conversion

MAPA supports pilots holding foreign licenses in the process of converting their licenses. An adapted program will be offered to you according to your needs and the requirements of the Moroccan civil aviation directorate. Following the request of the candidate wishing to convert his license, a commission meets at the invitation of the director of civil aeronautics. It studies the request for equivalence or validation of foreign licenses addressed to the civil aeronautics department in order to rule on each case and assess whether the foreign license and the associated qualifications are of a sufficient level to allow it to be awarded by equivalence. a corresponding Moroccan license or validation. Theoretical and practical aptitude tests may be decided by the commission.
